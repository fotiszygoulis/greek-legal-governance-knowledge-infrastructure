# Open Collaboration Note

## Greek Legal-Governance Knowledge Infrastructure for AI and Institutional Research

This repository is part of the **AGRIR-Lab** component **Greek Legal-Governance Knowledge Infrastructure for AI and Institutional Research** and is being developed as an exploratory methodological and research infrastructure space.

The project focuses on how Greek legal and administrative documents can be transformed from static PDF-based material into **structured, linked, and LLM-usable legal-governance corpora** that better support research on oversight, regulatory traceability, institutional analysis, governance-risk mapping, and AI-assisted public law research.

## Why Collaboration Is Relevant

This initiative sits at the intersection of:

- legal text structuring
- document parsing and transformation workflows
- legal-governance corpus design
- Greek public law and administrative law
- governance and institutional analysis
- AI-supported research infrastructure

Because of this interdisciplinary character, meaningful progress benefits from collaboration across methodological, legal, technical, and analytical perspectives.

## Areas Where Collaboration Is Welcome

Collaboration is especially welcome in the following areas:

### 1. Methodological Exchange
Discussion on corpus design, segmentation logic, document structuring, metadata models, linking strategies, and traceability-oriented workflows.

### 2. Legal-Governance Use Cases
Joint thinking on how the corpus can support institutional oversight analysis, competence mapping, compliance architectures, and public-sector governance research.

### 3. Public Law and Administrative Law Input
Advisory input on legal interpretation boundaries, competence structures, legal relevance of document categories, and risks of over-simplification in machine-usable legal corpora.

### 4. Technical and Workflow Support
Ideas related to parsing pipelines, markdown transformation, interlinking logic, repository design, sample corpus preparation, and reproducible research workflows.

### 5. Pilot Corpus Co-Development
Exploration of a bounded pilot corpus focused on oversight, accountability, administrative competence, or governance-related legal traceability.

## Current Stage of the Project

The repository is currently at an **early but structured stage**.

At present, the focus is on:

- conceptual framing
- methodological documentation
- use-case articulation
- pilot-oriented preparation
- collaboration readiness

The project does **not** currently claim to offer a finalized legal information system, a complete national legal corpus, or authoritative legal interpretation. Its purpose is to build a serious and usable research infrastructure pathway.

## What Kind of Engagement Is Most Useful

The most useful forms of engagement at this stage include:

- comments on methodological clarity
- suggestions for pilot scope
- examples of governance-relevant legal corpora
- discussion of linking and traceability logic
- lightweight collaboration on samples, structure, or use cases
- academic dialogue around public law, governance, and AI-supported legal research

## Contact and Research Orientation

This repository is intended as an open and serious research-oriented space for those interested in the transformation of Greek legal and administrative documents into more analytically usable knowledge structures.

Potential contributors, collaborators, or interlocutors may come from legal-tech, AI and law, public administration, institutional analysis, administrative law, regulatory studies, or open research infrastructure communities.

## Closing Note

This project is best understood as an invitation to **methodological dialogue and bounded collaboration** rather than as a finished system. Its value lies in building a careful pathway through which Greek legal-governance material can become more navigable, more traceable, and more useful for rigorous institutional and governance research.
