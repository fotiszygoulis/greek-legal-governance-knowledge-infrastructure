# Research Use Cases

## Greek Legal-Governance Knowledge Infrastructure for AI and Institutional Research

The value of a structured and interlinked legal-governance corpus lies not only in improved document accessibility, but in its capacity to support specific forms of research that would otherwise remain fragmented, time-consuming, or methodologically inconsistent. Within the context of the Algorithmic Governance, Risk & Institutional Resilience Lab (AGRIR-Lab), the transformation of Greek legal and administrative texts into linked, machine-usable knowledge structures is relevant insofar as it enhances the study of governance systems, institutional competences, compliance arrangements, oversight architectures, and public-sector resilience.

This note outlines the principal research use cases for such a knowledge infrastructure. These use cases are not intended as exhaustive categories, nor as claims of full operational maturity. Rather, they define the analytical directions in which a linked Greek legal-governance corpus may provide meaningful methodological support.

## 1. Institutional Oversight Analysis

One of the most immediate applications of a linked legal-governance corpus concerns the analysis of institutional oversight systems. Oversight in the public sector rarely depends on a single legal text. It is typically distributed across statutes, implementing acts, procedural instruments, organizational rules, competence clauses, and sector-specific regulations. As a result, the legal basis of supervisory authority, control mechanisms, accountability pathways, and reporting obligations is often dispersed and difficult to reconstruct systematically.

A structured corpus can assist by making visible the legal architecture through which oversight functions are constituted and exercised. It enables the identification of which bodies possess supervisory competences, under what legal basis they act, how those competences are limited or conditioned, and where institutional overlap or ambiguity may arise.

## 2. Regulatory Traceability

A second major use case concerns regulatory traceability. In governance research, it is often necessary to determine how legal obligations, procedures, definitions, and competence assignments travel across documents and institutional levels. Static archives make this difficult, especially where legal references are dense, indirect, or distributed across numerous acts.

A linked corpus improves the ability to trace these normative relationships. It allows the researcher to follow how a procedural obligation introduced in one text is implemented in another, how a competence provision depends on a broader statutory framework, or how an institutional responsibility is modified through subsequent amendments or related instruments.

## 3. Governance-Risk Mapping

A third application lies in governance-risk mapping. Legal and institutional risk does not arise only from explicit illegality or formal non-compliance. It may also emerge from structural features of the legal-governance environment, including fragmentation of competences, normative duplication, procedural ambiguity, weak coordination mechanisms, or unclear accountability lines.

By rendering legal relationships more visible, a structured and interlinked corpus may support the identification of governance-related risk patterns. For example, it can help reveal where multiple institutions appear to hold partially overlapping competences, where implementation chains depend on unclear procedural transitions, or where the legal basis of action is scattered across incompatible instruments.

## 4. AI-Assisted Public Law Research

A further use case concerns AI-assisted public law research. The expanding use of large language models and retrieval-supported systems in legal and policy analysis has created new opportunities, but also serious methodological risks. When source material is poorly structured, insufficiently segmented, or weakly linked, AI-supported retrieval may become shallow, decontextualized, or misleading.

A linked legal-governance corpus improves the quality of the source layer upon which AI-assisted inquiry depends. It creates conditions for more context-sensitive retrieval, more meaningful grounding in legal provisions, and better preservation of the relationships between norms, procedures, and institutions.

## 5. Administrative Competence Mapping

Another important research use case is administrative competence mapping. Many governance problems ultimately depend on institutional competence: who may act, within which limits, under what procedure, and in relation to which other bodies. In practice, competence allocation is often dispersed across constitutive statutes, organizational provisions, sectoral regulations, ministerial decisions, and internal administrative arrangements.

A linked corpus can support the systematic mapping of competence structures by connecting legal provisions that define, delimit, delegate, or operationalize institutional authority. Such mapping is valuable not only for organizational analysis, but also for the study of administrative legality, jurisdictional conflict, accountability design, and implementation capacity.

## 6. Policy and Case-Based Governance Analysis

A final use case concerns the support of case-based and policy-oriented governance analysis. Research on public administration, institutional reform, compliance failure, oversight breakdown, or governance response often requires a careful reconstruction of the relevant legal environment. Without a structured corpus, this reconstruction tends to be labor-intensive and vulnerable to omissions.

A legal-governance knowledge infrastructure can improve this process by providing a more navigable basis for case documentation and policy interpretation. It may help identify the legal framework surrounding a given administrative dispute, institutional intervention, compliance issue, or reform initiative.

## Concluding Perspective

Taken together, these use cases show that the relevance of a Greek legal-governance knowledge infrastructure extends well beyond technical document conversion. Its importance lies in its capacity to support structured institutional inquiry across multiple domains central to AGRIR-Lab: oversight, traceability, competence analysis, governance risk, compliance, and AI-assisted public law research.

At the same time, these use cases should be understood within the exploratory character of the component. A linked corpus enhances access, relational visibility, and analytical tractability, but it does not eliminate the need for legal interpretation, contextual judgment, or institutional expertise. Its role is infrastructural and methodological: to create a better-organized environment for serious governance research.
